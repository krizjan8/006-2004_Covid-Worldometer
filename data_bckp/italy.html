  • 1,221 new cases and 195 new deaths in Italy. Italy today has started to gradually reopen after nearly 2 months of a lockdown that was in place since March 11, the first and longest in Europe. "It's not over. If the infections rise, in 2 weeks we will have to close again," warned Ricciardi, Minister of Health's emergency adviser.

    After a few isolated cases detected in late January, the outbreak in Italy started on Feb. 21 [see archived news]. On  February 22, as Italy had already become the country with the highest number of cases among all non-Asian nations.11 towns and areas affected by the outbreak were placed in lockdown. A country-wide lockdown was implemented after 19 days, on March 11, when 827 deaths had already been reported. 

    On February 27, Worldometer had calculated and reported that about 60% of new cases (31 out of 54) diagnosed in Europe outside of Italy from Feb. 24 to Feb. 27 had recently traveled to Northern Italy.

    On February 28, the US CDC issued a travel warning to Italy: avoid all nonessential travel to Italy. [source]
